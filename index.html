Project Overview

This web-based simulator demonstrates the principles of projectile motion through an interactive interface. It allows users to visualize and experiment with various parameters that influence the motion of a projectile. The application is designed to help users understand the relationship between initial conditions such as velocity, angle, height, and gravitational acceleration, and how they affect the trajectory of the projectile.

Instructions

The simulation allows the user to modify the following input parameters:

  • Initial Height: The height from which the projectile is launched (in meters).
  • Initial Speed: The velocity at which the projectile is launched (in meters per second).
  • Launch Angle: The angle at which the projectile is launched (in degrees).
  • Mass: The mass of the projectile (in kilograms).
  • Gravitational Acceleration: The gravitational acceleration constant (in meters per second squared).

Users can also choose the slow motion option to view the simulation at a slower pace. This is particularly useful for closely examining the trajectory at different stages of the motion.

Features

  • Interactive Controls: Adjust initial conditions and observe the resulting motion in real-time.
  • Visual Representation: The trajectory of the projectile is displayed on a canvas, with real-time updates as the simulation progresses.
  • Multiple Display Options: View data related to position, velocity, acceleration, force, or energy of the projectile.
  • Reset/Start/Pause: Control the simulation by starting, pausing, or resetting the projectile to its initial position.

Simulation Details

The effect of air resistance is currently neglected in this simulation. The projectile follows a parabolic trajectory, with calculations based on basic kinematic equations.

This tool is designed for educational purposes and aims to provide a visual and interactive way to learn and explore the dynamics of projectile motion.
